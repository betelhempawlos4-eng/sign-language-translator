Getting started

How SignBridge works

Up and running in under 60 seconds — no downloads, no installs, no account needed.

1
Select your language
Choose your country. Each country has its own independent sign language with unique hand shapes, grammar, and vocabulary.
2
Start the camera
Click Start Camera and allow access. Position your hand clearly in frame with good lighting and a plain background behind you.
3
Sign steadily
Hold each sign for 1–2 seconds. The AI smooths detections across 5 frames so only stable, clear signs get added to your sentence.
4
Read, speak, share
Your translation builds automatically. Tap Speak to hear it aloud, Copy to paste it anywhere, or clear and start a new conversation.

Create one class per sign. Hold each sign steadily while Teachable Machine captures samples from your webcam.
Suggested signs to start with:

📖 Tips for best detection

1Good lighting: Sit facing a window or lamp. Avoid light sources behind you.
2Plain background: A plain wall helps MediaPipe track your hand clearly.
3Hold steady: Each sign needs to be stable for ~1 second across 5 frames before it registers.
4Distance: Keep your hand 30–60cm from the camera.
5Practice first: Use the Alphabet tab to compare your hand to the illustrated guides.

🌍 About sign languages

Sign languages are complete, independent languages with their own grammar, syntax, and vocabulary. They evolved naturally within deaf communities.

ASL and BSL are mutually unintelligible — despite both coming from English-speaking countries, they share almost no signs. LIBRAS (Brazil) has over 3 million users.

There are approximately 300 sign languages worldwide, many of which are endangered.
